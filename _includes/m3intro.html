Introduction to Modernization and Migration Management (M3)

Transitions are difficult but can be successful with sufficient planning and follow-through. Modernization and Migration Management (M3) is a framework to help agencies achieve successful outcomes and reduce risk during administrative system and/or service modernizations and migrations.

Download the M3 Playbook

What is the M3 Framework?

The M3 Framework segments modernizations and migrations into discrete phases. Each phase includes a series of objectives and recommended activities that are groups across four workstreams.

What is the M3 Playbook?

The Playbook is guidance and tools to help agencies plan for and navigate a transition. It was developed based on feedback from over 100 Government and industry experts, reflects best practices and lessons learned from prior migrations and is updated annually.

70+ Activities: Descriptions for how to apply the M3 framework, including detailed activity steps, inputs, outputs, lessons learned, and stakeholders involved.

20+ Templates: Documents that aid customers and providers in the completion of activities, and provide key considerations.

Supporting Guidance: Additional background support for each activity.

Sample Artifacts: An OMB Max page that includes sample documentation from previous modernization and migration projects.

How does the Sharing Quality Services new vision affect M3?

OMB has designated Quality Service Management Offices (QSMOs) for specific functional areas. The QSMOs will offer and manage competitive solutions that drive standardization, integrate solutions, and respond to agency business needs for centralized capabilities that have agreed upon standards.

For the purposes of this framework, customers of the QSMO should consider the term “provider” throughout the M3 activities, as the responsibility of the QSMO. Where a mandate to use the QSMO exists, Program Management activities related to procurement and selecting a provider (Activities 1.4, 2.1, and 3.3) may need to be altered. Updates to M3 will be made in the coming months during the annual update.
