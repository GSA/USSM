name: Define Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

objective: Define the level of service standards provided to the customer during Operations and Maintenance (O&M).
responsibleParty: customer
category: process

activities: 
  - text: Review Target State Concept of Operations and roles and responsibilities for O&M services
    responsibleParty: shared
  - text: Review and discuss existing provider service level metrics to determine if there need to be adjustments. All service level metrics must align, and not conflict with, the metrics included in the <a href="https://www.ussm.gov/fibf/">QSMO Performance Management Framework</a>
    responsibleParty: shared
  - text: Identify additional service level metrics based on objective, clearly-defined, and measurable criteria, in alignment with the <a href="https://www.ussm.gov/fibf/">QSMO Performance Management Framework</a>
    responsibleParty: shared
  - text: Gain stakeholder buy-in on metrics that will be used post-migration to measure O&M performance as part of the performance management strategy
    responsibleParty: shared
  - text: Define performance management capabilities, processes, and tools
    responsibleParty: shared
  - text: Finalize service level metrics, targets, and remediation or issue resolution policies, in alignment with the <a href="https://www.ussm.gov/fibf/">QSMO Performance Management Framework</a> 
    responsibleParty: shared
  - text: Report updates in governance meetings, and Status Reports/Dashboards, informing QSMOs as necessary
    responsibleParty: shared

inputs:
  - Target State Concept of Operations 
  - Provider Agreement
  - <a href="https://ussm.gsa.gov/fibf/">Federal Integrated Business Framework (FIBF)</a> Performance Measures 

outputs:
  - SLAs
  - Status Reports/Dashboards

      - customer
      - provider

bestPractice:
  - Include services and systems with identified target metrics in your SLAs 
  - Review Provider Agreements, SLAs, and Life Cycle Cost Estimate (LCCEs) for any potential updates that may be required
  - Set up the SLA to foster a mutually beneficial relationship. Make sure that both the provider and customer are clear on expectations of success defined in Activity 1.6
